# OVOZ — Pitch Deck Script (v01)

**For:** Oppoq (designer)
**Audience:** Hackathon judges who likely don't speak Mandarin
**Goal:** Impressive + Unforgettable
**Length:** ~60–90 seconds verbal, ~10 slides

---

## Design Direction (Not Negotiable)

OVOZ is a **clinical diagnostic instrument**, not a language app. Every slide should feel like medical imaging or a Bloomberg terminal. Not Duolingo. Not Babbel. Not playful. Forensic.

**Color system:**
- Background: deep black `#0a0a0a`
- Type: pure white `#FFFFFF`
- Signal red `#FF3838` — the diagnosis, the error, the L1 fingerprint
- Metallic gold `#D4A437` — the corrected voice, the resolution

**Typography:**
- Display: heavy, condensed sans (Druk, Termina, NB Akademie Std bold). Compressed letterforms read like data.
- Body: monospace or technical sans (JetBrains Mono, Söhne Mono). One typeface for "this is being measured."
- Mandarin: SF Pro Display SC or a high-quality CJK serif for the rare moments characters appear. Big.

**Grid:** Strict 12-column Müller-Brockmann. Generous margins. Single idea per slide.

**Motion:** Sharp cuts between slides. No fades, no springs. Cuts feel like a procedure.

---

## The Deck Arc (10 Slides)

The arc is engineered for non-Mandarin-speaking judges to trust three verifiable signals:
1. **L1 detection** (they know the user's L1, so they verify)
2. **Timbre match** (their ears confirm same voice)
3. **Lip alignment** (visual proof)

These three are the **trust ladder**. Every slide reinforces at least one rung.

### THE LIVE DEMO (60 seconds)

This is not slides. This is the app on screen. Choreograph it:

**Seconds 0–8:** On-stage user speaks the demo sentence in Mandarin. Badly. Live phoneme waveform shows.

**Seconds 8–10:** ANALYZING state. Tense, clinical. Phoneme grid teases.

**Seconds 10–15:** ⚡ **THE HERO MOMENT.** Diagnosis card slams full-screen:
> RUSSIAN L1 DETECTED
> Palatalization on /zh/

In signal red. Three seconds held. The room goes quiet.

*(Optional belief amplifier: have a planted native Mandarin speaker in the audience say loud enough to hear: "That's exactly what they did.")*

**Seconds 15–22:** GOLDEN VOICE plays. The same person's timbre — but now saying the Mandarin perfectly. Waveform animates in gold. The audience hears the same voice they just heard fail, now succeed.

**Seconds 22–35:** Lip mirror engages. Webcam shows the user's lips next to the target lip shape. They mimic. The two shapes lock together.

**Seconds 35–40:** Green resolution. Done.

│                                 │
│   RUSSIAN L1 DETECTED           │ ← signal red
│                                 │
│   Palatalization on /zh/        │ ← white

│                                 │
│   Chen et al. Interspeech 2013  │ ← micro mono
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘

This card needs to be designed at poster quality. It is the screenshot people will share. Make it look like a clinical reading. **Heavy condensed type. Signal red on black. A single research citation in the corner.** No icons, no decoration.

If this card is right, you have won the deck before the golden voice even plays.

## What Sells The Non-Mandarin-Speaking Judges (The Belief Stack)

They cannot judge whether the Mandarin is good. They CAN judge these three signals:

| Rung | What they verify | Slide / Moment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **L1 detection** is correct | Demo second 10, diagnosis card |
| 2 | **Voice timbre** is the same person | Demo second 15, golden voice |
| 3 | **Lip alignment** is closing the gap | Demo second 22, mirror |

Plant a friendly native Mandarin speaker in the audience. After the golden voice plays, have them say audibly: *"That's native level."* This is the legal cheat. Borrow someone else's authority for free.

## Speaker Notes (Tone)

- Speak slower than you want to. The room is louder than you think.
- Pause after the L1 diagnosis card lands. Three seconds of silence is the most powerful sound in the room.
- Do not explain the technology. Show it.
- The phrase "your voice, working" is your tagline. Use it at the start, in the demo, at the close.
- Don't apologize for anything that breaks. If something fails, say "this is hackathon code" once, then keep going.

If you only have 45 seconds: drop Slides 3, 8, and 9. Keep 1 (hook), 4 (moat), 5 (product), 6 (watch), DEMO, 7 (recap), 10 (close).

If you only have 30 seconds: drop slides entirely. Open with "Watch." Run the demo. Close with "OVOZ. Your voice. Working. Tashkent to world."

The demo is the deck. The deck is the cushion.
